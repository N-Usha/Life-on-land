Endangered Mammals

The Javan Rhinoceros

The animal is extinct due to poaching, mainly for their horns, which are highly used in traditional Chinese medicine. (A mainland subspecies of the Javan rhino was declared extinct in Vietnam in 2011)

Status - Critically Endangered , Current count - 72

Weight - 1,400 kgs to 2,000 kilograms. Length-including its head is 2 to 4 metres

Gestation period - 16 Month , No. of young - one calf at a time.

Lifespan -30-40 years

Diet - variety of plants, particularly their shoots, twigs, and fallen fruit.

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests such as dense rainforests with mud wallows and plenty of water

Mountain Gorillas

The primary threat to mountain gorillas comes from forest clearance and degradation, as the region's growing human population struggles to eke out a living

Status - Critically Endangered , Current count 700 individuals.

Weight -150kg , Height -4-6 feet tall

Gestation period - 8.5 months , No. of young 2.

Lifespan-35 years

Diet - Leaves, stems, bark, roots, flowers

Habitat: Mountains with broadleaf, evergreen, subtropical rainforests

Asian Elephant

It is threatened by loss of habitat, habitat degradation and poaching for its tusks.

Status - Endangered , Current count-40,000-50,000 elephant

Weight- -4,000 to 2,700 kg , Height-2.7-2.2 m at shoulder

Gestation period - 19 and 22 months , No. of young 1 to 2 calves per birth .

Lifespan-10 to 12 years

Diet - grasses, fruit, and bark

Habitat- grasslands, tropical evergreen forests, semi-evergreen forests, moist deciduous forests, dry deciduous forests and dry thorn forests.

Saola

Saola is threatened primarily by hunting, rather than habitat loss. The main hunting threat comes from commercial poaching, not subsistence hunting by local people.

Status - Critically Endangered , Current count- 750.

Weight 220 pounds , Height 59 to 77 inches

Gestation period 8 months , No. of young 1.

Lifespan-8 to 11 years

Diet - Trees , grass , nuts , barks, berries

Habitat wet evergreen or deciduous forests in eastern Indochina, preferring rivers and valleys
